# RFP Accelerator — Consolidated Requirements

## Business Goal & Scenario
- Shorten the cycle time required to respond to RFPs by automating upstream tasks
- Upstream tasks targeted for automation:
  - Analyze incoming RFPs for missing details and risks, and support bid/no-bid decisions
  - Find the correct RFP reference document for grounding
  - Generate structured, grounded first-draft response data

## Success Criteria
- Measurable hours saved per RFP/project
- Increased accuracy of responses
- Increased appropriate verbosity/completeness of responses

## Current Technical Blockers
- Ingestion document limit is currently 50 pages per ingest; must support up to 200 pages (most documents fall in the 50–120 page range)
- Questions not reliably mapping to an answer during generation
- Need to expand the breadth of knowledge references available for grounding

## System Architecture — Two Systems

### System 1: Knowledge Base (Questions & Approved Answers)
- Repository of common questions and approved answers
- Standard KB content lifecycle: submit → review → approve → retire
- KB structured "per question" (one KB entry per question)
- Mechanism to map "similar questions" to an existing KB entry rather than generating a new response
- Scheduled content reviews: route content to a list of reviewers on a recurring cadence (every X weeks/months)
- Content generated by the LLM (System 2) must enter the KB approval process before being approved and reused
- KB answer versioning:
  - Answers change over time and must be versioned
  - Each RFP retains a reference to the specific answer version it used
  - A version may not be deleted while it is still referenced by an RFP

### System 2: RFP Reader & Response Generator
- Ingests the incoming RFP and decomposes it into sections / subsections / questions (configurable granularity)
- Looks up answers in the KB and annotates which KB entry was used (source provenance)
- Fallback when no KB match is found:
  - Search a configurable list of "trusted web sources"
  - Use an LLM to summarize the retrieved content
  - Route any LLM-generated content into the KB approval process for approval and potential reuse

## Collaboration & Workflow
- Users can "save and come back to" an in-progress RFP
- Users can collaborate on an RFP
- Deep linking for email / Teams references (link directly to an RFP, section, or question)

## Document / Format Support
- Common RFP response formats to support: PDF, DOCX, XLSX, MD, TXT
- Ingestion must scale to 200-page documents

## Audit, Retention & Versioning
- Audit capabilities across both the KB and RFP systems: who changed what and when
- RFP retention period of 2 years; suggest "archiving" RFPs after that period
- KB answer versioning (see System 1) with referential integrity protecting versions still in use

## Authentication & Roles
- Active Directory authentication must be supported
- Distinct roles required:
  - RFP users
  - RFP approvers
  - KB users
  - KB editors
  - KB approvers

## Open Questions
- **Version deletion behavior:** When a still-referenced answer version needs to be removed, is deletion blocked outright, or is there a "soft-retire" state (unusable for new RFPs, still readable for historical ones)?
- **"Similar question" matching:** What similarity threshold triggers a match, and is there a human-override/confirmation path before reusing an existing KB entry?
- **KB conflict resolution:** How are two approved answers to near-identical questions reconciled? Is there a canonical/preferred entry?
- **Concurrent collaboration:** What is the locking/merge model when multiple users edit the same RFP or section simultaneously?
- **Bid/no-bid output:** Is the analysis advisory only, or does it gate the workflow? Who owns the decision and is it recorded?
- **Trusted web sources governance:** Who maintains the trusted-source list, and how often is it reviewed? Any per-RFP override?
- **Confidence/provenance display:** Should generated answers surface a confidence score or flag KB-grounded vs. LLM-generated content to the end user?
- **Archiving semantics:** After 2 years, does "archived" mean read-only, moved to cold storage, or excluded from search? Can archived RFPs be restored?
- **Page-limit scaling:** What's driving the current 50-page cap (model context, ingestion pipeline, cost)? Does 200 pages require chunking strategy changes?
- **Review cadence ownership:** Who assigns reviewers and sets the per-content review interval — KB approvers, or a separate admin role?
- **AD role mapping:** Do the five application roles map to existing Active Directory groups, or are they managed separately within the app?
- **Format fidelity:** For XLSX/structured inputs, how are tabular questions decomposed, and must responses preserve the original document's format on export?
